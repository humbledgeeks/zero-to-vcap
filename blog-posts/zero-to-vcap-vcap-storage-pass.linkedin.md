There are moments in your career that remind you why the hard work is worth it.

Today was one of those moments.

I officially passed **3V0-23.25: Advanced VMware Cloud Foundation 9.0 Storage** — the VCAP-VCF Storage exam. 🎉

This wasn't a whim. It was several months of preparation — countless hours in my VCF 9.1 home lab, hundreds of hours of Broadcom training, a lot of documentation, and more late nights than I care to admit. Some of those hours were spent troubleshooting problems I broke on purpose, just so I'd actually understand them.

Advanced certs aren't about memorizing answers. They're about building the experience and confidence to solve problems you've never seen before.

The biggest advantage I had was the lab. Instead of reading about features, I deployed them, broke them, rebuilt them, upgraded them, migrated workloads, and tested failure scenarios until they made sense. And I documented it all in my Zero to VCAP series — because the best way to learn something is to teach it.

I don't want to hand someone a fish. I want to teach them how to fish.

When the result came back, I saw one word: PASS. Relief, excitement, validation, gratitude — all at once.

This is another milestone on the road to becoming a Broadcom Knight, but it's not the finish line. More to learn, more certs to earn, more blogs to write, more labs to build.

Thank you to my family, my coworkers and mentors, and everyone following along with Zero to VCAP. Your encouragement has meant more than you know.

On to the next challenge. 💪
